What is AHEC?

Area Health Education Center

The National AHEC Organization supports and advances the Area Health Education Center (AHEC) Network to improve health by leading the nation in recruitment, training and retention of a diverse health work force for underserved communities.

AHECs are designed:

  • To be responsive to local health needs and serve as an important link between academic training programs and community-based outreach programs
  • To provide innovative, collaborative and multi-disciplinary responses to community health issues.
  • Develop community health education training programs to meet the needs of the diverse and severely underserved populations.

Shore AHEC is part of the National AHEC Organization

Shore AHEC is an affiliated Program of Rowan School of Osteopathic Medicine.

Learning from Service

The goal of the community service learning experience is to provide students with the opportunity to learn about health care disparities, health care access issues, and where possible, improve the health care services in underserved communities. To facilitate learning and growth, a “learning-by-doing” opportunity by providing students with a chance to teach community members what they know, while simultaneously learning from them.

Student Doctor Community Contributions:

  • Our students provide over 3,000 service hours to the community
  • Coordinate and conduct Community Health Education and Health Prevention Screening activities.
